Government

-President elected by the people for terms of five years

-very powerful figure who is only limited in terms of appointments by having to appoint National Congresspeople for cabinet

-does not have a veto but sits on all sessions of the Board of One Hundred and Eight


-legislature is divided into National Congress and Board of One Hundred and Eight

-National Congress consists of 3,125 members elected direct by the people in individual constituencies

-essentially a dead assembly with few debates and largely mute

-for five year terms

-Board of One Hundred and Eight elected by super-constituencies (merged from National Congress constituencies) from already elected National Congresspeople

-may propose laws if supported by 36 members


-Board of Supervisors supervises functioning of all government

-appointed from among people who have served for at least fifteen years in National Congress

-for terms of twelve years

-done by co-option

-or else auto-admission if exam is passed

-or else by co-nomination of president and two-thirds of National Congress

-may lay charges against government officials and call byelections in National Congress

-may also exclude National Congresspeople from eligibility to the Board of One Hundred and Eight

-also supervises examinations and appointments to the civil service


-Supreme Council of Revision

-judicial body

-charges laid by Board of Supervisors are submitted in trial to it


-provinces run by prefects

-prefects are appointed by the president but require the consent of at least one third of the provincial councils
