United States of America

-there has been no National Prohibition here

-there was some prohibition under Constitution of the United States (1892) at the state level

-but there cannot be county-level prohibition

-only state with prohibition is Mississippi due to its Colored temperance movement leading the charge but frequently ignored in practice

Cider

-in OTL we'd call this hard cider

-most dominant form of alcohol in the US

-ahead of OTL thanks to a lot more British immigrants coming in

-and no apple orchards destroyed

-and amplifying surge of production as well

Beer

-very Germanized tradition

-a lot of beer gardens

-a lot of lager sold in particular

-at the same time there's a fair amount of farmale produced

-and specifically certified by the state level as part of a policy to promote such ale


-a great amount of ale gets sold in this era

Wine

-chief wine regions in the US are New York and Virginia

-New York has a very long tradition, but heavily influenced by German immigrants

-Virginia chiefly a byproduct of the Reconstruction era where it helps to enhance the state's economic boom

-other wine areas are

-Wisconsan, which had a lot of German and British immigrants who brought a lot of it

-and due to the state enacting reforms to produce it early on

-Michigan, which is a direct spinoff of the New York wine industry


-Riesling

-brought by German immigrants

-and as a result there's a huge volume of American production of it

-mostly grown in New York and, to a lesser extent, Virginia

-most consumed wine in the US

Whiskey

-high amounts of production

Moonshine

-with settlement of Kances and Nibrasca by a lot of Appalachian people they continue their moonshining traditions there

-this results in traditions being brought to the surface and becomes a very influential brand

-spreads gradually across Middle West as both this comes to surface and also due to Appalachian settlement towards it

-and virtually all Buenaventuran mezcal is in the shadow of bacanora

Wine

-Chileans played founding role in wine industry here

Pisco

-a type of grape brandy produced from grapes from country

-brought over by Chileans

-most influential in North California

Whiskey

-brought by large numbers of Southern US migrants

-leads to, in particular, a Texan whiskey brand becoming a big thing

Sangaree

-popular drink in southern US, brought over by migrants

-coming to south from west indies

-drink declined and ceased to exist in southern US proper but lives on in Buenaventura
